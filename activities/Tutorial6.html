Introduction to the Greatest Philosophers of All Time



Spinoza is, objectively, the greatest philosopher of all time. He is loved by many others, including Nietzsche, George Eliot, and Mark Jerusalem. He believed that God=Nature and that the meaning of life is to love God-aka-Nature, i.e., to strive for the flourishing of the whole Universe, which flourishes most of all via people.
"We are a way for the Cosmos to know Itself" - Carl Sagan

Will MacAskill, along with Toby Ord, cofounded the movement called Effective Altruism. In 2022, he wrote a book arguing in favour of longtermism—the idea that safeguarding future generations is a key moral priority—called What We Owe the Future



In 2020, Toby Ord wrote his masterpiece The Precipice, which outlined the main existential risks facing Humanity in the 21st century, explained why it's a moral imperative to reduce existential risk and explained how to do so.

Nietzsche was a 19th century German philosopher who thought that most people were implicitly nihilists (and prioritised a non-existent Heaven/Beyond/God/Nirvana/Paradise over Reality itself), and advocated something similar to Spinoza's Love of Reality/God/Nature as a solution to nihilism.
